Global Engagements

Nigeria

Organizations: ILO, Federal Government of Nigeria

Focus Areas: Employment policy, labour markets, skills systems, institutional reform

Role: Principal Consultant / Lead Policy Expert

Key Activities

  • Led the design, review, and reform of Nigeria’s National Employment Policy (2025) under the auspices of ILO Nigeria, ensuring alignment with labour market realities, skills demand, and inclusive growth priorities.
  • Served as Principal Consultant for the review of the National Employment Policy, coordinating technical inputs and collaboration among government ministries, social partners, and relevant international development agencies.
  • Conducted national labour market diagnostic studies assessing employment trends, informality, youth employment, private-sector absorption capacity, and structural labour market challenges in Nigeria.
  • Led a nationwide employment mapping field research and reporting project for the ILO, generating evidence to inform employment and labour market policy decisions.
  • Produced an Employment and Labour Market Diagnostic Study of Nigeria to support policy formulation and programme design.
  • Carried out research on employment and livelihood promotion in rural Nigeria, with a focus on labour market conditions and the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on jobs and livelihoods (2023).
  • Provided technical guidance on skills development, TVET alignment, and apprenticeship systems to support workforce development and employability.
  • Led the mapping of stakeholder roles, relationships, and engagement dynamics within Nigeria’s TVET and apprenticeship systems (SEESIN Project, 2025).
  • Authored policy briefs on strengthening TVET and apprenticeship systems in Nigeria as pathways to skills development, job creation, and inclusive growth (SEESIN Project, 2025).
  • Produced a policy forum synthesis report on Nigeria’s apprenticeship system, consolidating evidence, stakeholder perspectives, and reform options (SEESIN Project, 2025).
  • Developed evidence-based findings and strategic recommendations for transforming Nigeria’s TVET and apprenticeship systems.
  • Mapped stakeholders and resource mobilization opportunities to support the implementation of Nigeria’s Decent Work Country Programme III (2024).
  • Supported institutional coordination across ministries, labour market institutions, and social partners to improve coherence in employment, skills, and labour policies.
